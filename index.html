Age of geneva

Many great projects show the age of cities around the world using the construction dates of their building stock. These inspired me to do the same for Geneva. Oh, and the map is interactive! The user can choose a particular date-range of interest

Geographical names

of Geneva's streets

Illustrious families, inspiring female figures or influential men of the past have been chosen to designate the streets of Geneva. This responsive and open-source map interactively presents the layout of these streets. Its goal is to show unequal gender treatment.

Rencontre

Alain Monney, a Swiss artist, creates Geotags. This concept consists in a one-stroke “drawing” using roads, bridges and existing trails in a certain area. For this project, Alain Monney travelled to Rencontre, a lieu-dit in the region of Ardèche, France, where he worked on 25 such figures. My job was to digitize them and to create a story map.

Kiswaro water project

"Water is life" - the slogan of the association H2O-Energies! This group is active in providing potable water to populations in Cameroon and Kenya. In my capacity as volunteer GIS consultant, I proudly work with the team and support their efforts. One of my tasks was to design this map, which situates the future water pipelines in the region of Kiswaro, Kenya.

Renaturation of the Seymaz

During my internship for the State of Geneva’s Office of Geomatics and Data Management, I created numerous web applications and story maps with ArcGIS Online. Here's one of my favorites, because it speaks about nature and how efficient policies can help protect it. The idea is that the user follows the stream of the Seymaz river by scrolling down the content and stopping at every restoration spot.

Contact me

The topic of Geographical Information Systems always intrigued me and throughout my studies I became increasingly captivated by the opportunities it presented. Starting with a Bachelor of Arts in Geography, Journalism and Sociology from the University of Neuchâtel (UNINE), I then completed my Master’s at the University of Geneva (UNIGE) in Environmental Sciences with a specialization in climatology. My thesis focused on urban heat islands in the city of Geneva. Finally, convinced of my interest for GIS, I completed the Geomatics Certificate at UNIGE.  This being a constantly evolving subject, I always like to try new ways of working with GIS tools and improve my skills.
